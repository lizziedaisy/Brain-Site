About Me


I am 23 years old (Taurus baby), super funsized, British born, currently in Amsterdam.

I love food, gin, animals, travelling, photography, hiking, and books.

I hate rum, Kanye West, people being needy, people being shitty, disappointing food, being too cold, and books with bad endings.

The Beginning

April 2015

University is over! I have a first class honours degree, and a few months of freedom. Will be moving to Madrid this year.

May 2015

I'm on holiday in Ireland with my brother in law and the family, and I wake up in hospital after having a huge seizure in my sleep. Referred to epilepsy clinic at home and dismissed.

May 2015

I am waiting for my epilepsy appointment when I have a huge brain haemorrhage. My left side stops working, and my boyfriend ignores my "I'm sure it's nothing" attitude and takes me to hospital.

May 2015

It's not just a bleed: it's a brain tumour. A nice round golf ball, bleeding all over my brain. I start on steroids, which are disgusting. I sleep most of the time.

June 2015

I am about to graduate. I sleep all the time, and I can't drink. We have tried more treatments, none of which have worked. Grad ball is crap for me. We're going for plan X.

August 2015

On the 10th August, it is surgery day. We have just frantically moved house and changed job plans. Glasgow will be home for a while.

The Middle

The Surgery - Before

August 10th at 8am. My surgeon sent me a text yesterday 'back from my holiday, see you tomorrow! All will be fine!'. I'm not sure if it's weird or reassuring. Probably both. My hair is gone. I felt sick. My hair upset me more than surgery. I have zero self confidence and my hair has been my best feature for years. Now it's gone. I look like a Sick Person. Tristan offered to shave his head too, but I think my heart woud break every day looking at him. I'm avoiding mirrors.

The Surgery - After

I woke up. I woke up, and I have toes and fingers that move, and I can see out of my eyes, and I can talk. No feeling will ever top that feeling, for the rest of my life. I get hourly morphine and never sleep because everything is a blur. My nurses are incredible human beings, although my ward is full of grumpy rude old people. I hate old people (sorry, old people).

The Ending... what I know now:


- Most friends are not real friends
- Pregnancy pillows are great if the back of your head is a gaping scar
- Get a Netflix subscription
- Yes, the kettle is too heavy to lift and fill at first
- Accept help anywhere and everywhere
- Giant scars on bald heads will make people stare
- Strangers will feel entitled to ask you questions
- Children may point and cry
- Staples do not hurt to remove, but it's still not fun to sit through
- Alcohol and epilepsy drugs do not mix
- Stairs are an exhausting activity
- Lying down flat is a true luxury
- Comfort food is necessary
- Tell people what you need - they aren't mind readers

- When you are lucky enough to have a few magic humans in your life, hold onto them
